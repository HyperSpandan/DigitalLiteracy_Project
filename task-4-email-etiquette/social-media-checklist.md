# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**File:** social-media-checklist.md
**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | VIT Bhopal University

---

## ✅ DO's – Responsible Social Media Use

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|----------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, decent photo and a real name on platforms like LinkedIn. | Employers and internship coordinators screen candidates online. |
| 2 | **Set personal accounts to Private** — On Instagram, Snapchat, and similar platforms, restrict who can see your content. | Protects your personal data and limits exposure to strangers. |
| 3 | **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" | Posts are permanent. Even deleted content can be screenshot and shared. |
| 4 | **Share accurate information only** — Verify news before sharing or reposting. | Sharing misinformation damages your credibility and can cause real harm. |
| 5 | **Use social media to build your brand** — Share your projects, achievements, and learning milestones on LinkedIn or GitHub. | Demonstrates initiative and creates a positive digital footprint. |
| 6 | **Engage respectfully in online discussions** — Disagree constructively, avoid personal attacks, and maintain a civil tone. | Online behaviour reflects your character and is visible to your network. |
| 7 | **Report harmful or abusive content** — Use built-in reporting tools on platforms when you witness cyberbullying or harassment. | Helps maintain safer online communities for everyone. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts – What to Avoid

| # | Don't | Why It's Harmful |
|---|-------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Don't post offensive, abusive, or discriminatory content** — This includes jokes, memes, or comments targeting any group. | Can result in disciplinary action from your college and damage future career prospects. |
| 2 | **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, class timetable, or daily routine. | Makes you vulnerable to stalking, phishing, and identity theft. |
| 3 | **Don't engage with or forward suspicious messages** — Chain messages, prize-winning notifications, or requests for your OTP/bank details. | Common tactics used in online scams and phishing attacks. |
| 4 | **Don't use social media to vent about college, faculty, or classmates** — Even vague or indirect posts can be identified and traced back to you. | Can lead to disciplinary proceedings and irreparable professional relationships. |
| 5 | **Don't impersonate others or create fake accounts** — Creating fake profiles of classmates or public figures is a legal offence under the IT Act, 2000. | Punishable by law; constitutes cybercrime. |
| 6 | **Don't ignore privacy settings** — Many platforms default to maximum visibility. Never leave settings unchecked after creating an account. | Strangers and data brokers can harvest your data without your knowledge. |
