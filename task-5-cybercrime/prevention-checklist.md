## 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

> **Remember:** Entering your UPI PIN always means MONEY GOING OUT — never to receive money.

---

### General Cyber Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — at least 12 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free).

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps. This adds a second layer even if your password is stolen.

- [ ] **3. Never click on links in suspicious SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages** — especially those claiming you've won a prize, need to verify your account, or owe a fine.

- [ ] **4. Keep your apps and phone OS updated** — security patches in updates fix known vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

- [ ] **5. Do not use public Wi-Fi for banking or sensitive logins** — use your mobile data or a VPN if necessary.

- [ ] **6. Regularly review app permissions** — revoke location, microphone, or contacts access for apps that don't need them.

---

### 💳 UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory for Indian Students)

- [ ] **7. Remember: YOU NEVER NEED YOUR PIN TO RECEIVE MONEY on UPI.** If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "accept" a payment, it is a scam. Hang up immediately.

- [ ] **8. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, CVV, or net banking password with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or customer care. Legitimate institutions never ask for these.

---

### Social & Identity Safety

- [ ] **9. Avoid oversharing on social media** — never post your phone number, address, college schedule, or travel plans publicly.

- [ ] **10. Verify before you trust** — if someone contacts you claiming to be a classmate, faculty, or recruiter, verify their identity through official channels before sharing any personal information or clicking links.

---

### If You Are a Victim — How to Report

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File complaints online for financial fraud, cyberbullying, and more |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (available 24×7) — Call immediately if you have lost money in an online fraud |
| 🏦 **Your Bank** | Immediately call your bank's helpline to freeze the transaction |
| 🏫 **College Authorities** | Report cyberbullying or harassment incidents to your institution's grievance cell |

> **Act fast.** Reporting within the first hour of a financial fraud gives the highest chance of recovering your money.
