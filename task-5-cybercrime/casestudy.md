## Case Study: "The Collect Request Trap" – A UPI Payment Fraud Scenario

### What Is UPI Payment Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into authorising money transfers from their bank accounts through manipulation, impersonation, or technical deception. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI attacks exploit the victim's own device and PIN, making them difficult to reverse.

### How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. **The Setup:** A first-year student, Arjun, posts on OLX trying to sell his old laptop for ₹15,000.
2. **The Contact:** A "buyer" calls Arjun, expresses interest, and says he will pay immediately via UPI.
3. **The Trick:** The fraudster sends Arjun a UPI "Collect Request" — a notification that looks like an incoming payment — for ₹15,000. He tells Arjun, "Just enter your UTP PIN to accept the payment."
4. **The Loss:** Arjun enters his PIN to "receive" the money. In reality, entering the PIN on a collect request authorises a payment OUT of his account. ₹15,000 is debited.
5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster disconnects. The number is unregistered. The money is gone.

### Who Is Usually Targeted?
Young adults, students, and first-time online sellers who are unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work. People in urgent need of money are also frequently targeted.

### Consequences
- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Emotional distress and erosion of trust in digital payment systems
- In some reported cases, fraudsters make multiple collect requests within seconds, draining accounts

### Source Reference
Similar incidents widely reported on NDTV, Times of India, and documented by RBI's cybersecurity advisories (rbi.org.in).
